ABOUT

Zenith Recording Studios is located in Echo Park, Los Angeles, Ca. Our team has over 15 years of experience whether the situation is audio, video, or stage production. We pride ourselves in top notch work with a finished product that will far exceed any expectations. Our live room features an open sound with movable gobos to create a dead sound as well. The studio also features a vibey iso room and spacious vocal booth. Contact us today for a tour.

Jed Gumiran

Facebook LinkedIn Pinterest

Jed grew up in a home surrounded by music. His parents encouraged him to take piano lessons at age 5. Jed took it upon himself to join the school band where he learned the clarinet and earned himself the 1st chair position at age 11. Also at 11, he picked up the guitar for the first time and began gigging almost immediately. Playing in different bands growing up led to his first studio experience at age 13. Intrigued by the process, Jed experimented with his 4-track cassette recorder in his parents garage and recorded a handful of bands throughout high school with a PC computer he built in his bedroom. At age 17, Jed had his first internship at a small recording studio in Riverside, Ca. called Love Juice Labs. Jed left the internship to make more time for his own creativity and music career in a band affiliated with Columbia Records. After touring the country a number of times, Jed decided to go to school to further his education on the art of recording. In recent years, Jed has produced electronic music and gigged with a number of bands. Today, Jed has skills ranging from producing, recording, motion graphics, and web design.

Ramey Robles

Facebook LinkedIn Pinterest

Ramey Robles has been both a cinephile and technophile for most of his life, but mostly he's always just been a tinkerer. At 1 1/2 he tinkered with the microwave until he could warm his own milk. At the age of 2, he tinkered with the VCR so that he could play his favorite video tape with a copy of Back to the Future and PeeWee's Big Adventure non-stop. These small tell-tale signs set a precedent for the rest of his life. In his teen years he spent sometime tinkering with music, starting with punk, and eventually maturing his musical abilities and musical tastes, although he has always held punk ethics close to his heart. That tinkering with music eventually led to touring around the country as both a roadie and a musician, opening his eyes to the ways that the rest of the country lived outside the small culture bubble of Southern California that he grew up in. While all this was going on, Ramey tinkered with websites for the bands he was in, seeing the websites he made an additional canvas for the representations that they wished to portray to the world. Ramey then went onto tinkering with videos, which led him to switch his major at the University of California Riverside from Creative Writing to Media and Cultural Studies with an emphasis in Documentary and Ethnography. In recent years, Ramey was tinkering around again, this time in the Internet industry, where he spent most of his time working on search engine optimization, social media, and current web standards and trends. Having spent the last few years in the Internet industry has allowed him to apply his tinkering towards a full-time career. His years of experience tinkering allow him to offer a wide-range of professional-level media production for those wishing to tell their story on through film, video event production, music videos, and web sites.
